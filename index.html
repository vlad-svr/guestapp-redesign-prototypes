What changed and why

Each improvement maps to a concrete gap found in the current redesign-2.0 code.

Home & check-in list

Focus

"Next up" spotlight on the checklist

Now in code: the pending row gets a blue badge ring, but all rows carry equal visual weight.

Proposed: the next task gets a left accent bar + soft blue wash + pulsing badge + "Next up" chip, and completed rows show outcome facts ("€ 380.00 paid"). One glance answers "what do I do now?".

Motivation

Arrival countdown & instant-keys incentive

Now in code: the hero shows dates/times; the reason to finish check-in early is implicit.

Proposed: a glass countdown strip in the hero — "Check-in opens in 3 days — finish 2 tasks to get your keys instantly" — tying tasks to the reward (virtual keys), which also appears as a "Locked" state on the keys card.

Discovery

"Handpicked for your trip" on Home

Now in code: Home spreads upsells across three disconnected surfaces — RecommendationsSection grid, a desktop-only UpsellCarousel in the aside, and a FreeOffersFab — each reading the same items list.

Proposed: one "Handpicked for your trip" section that mixes native offers (Add/View) with the Mozio and Airalo banners in a single grid, a "See all" into the extras flow, and a GuruWalk Free-offers FAB — the same sources, one coherent moment.

Sign in & onboarding

Orientation

The sign-up machine finally shows its steps

Now in code: LoginView runs a hidden state machine (email → password → check_email_code) with a single "Create account" title and no indication of how many steps remain — the guest doesn't know they're 1 of 3.

Proposed: each step names itself ("step 2 of 3"), the password step gets a show/hide toggle + strength meter, and the email-code step gets a 6-cell OTP with a resend countdown (the current resend is a bare always-on link with no cooldown).

Motivation

The account is sold, not just demanded

Now in code: the create-account screen asks for an email with no reason given; "continue without logging in" sits below as an equal, unexplained option — so guests can't weigh the choice.

Proposed: three plain-language value bullets (progress is saved · register everyone once · come back for keys & guides) frame why an account is worth 20 seconds, and "continue without an account" stays available but honest. On desktop these live in a persistent brand panel.

Onboarding

Radios & checkboxes become choice cards on a labelled rail

Now in code: the 4 onboarding steps use a bare segmented bar with no labels or numbers (OnboardingStepsProgress), plain bordered radios for reservation type, and text checkboxes for who's coming — no icons, no avatars.

Proposed: the rail shows "Your trip · Step 1 of 4"; reservation type becomes icon choice cards (family / group / tourist), guests use tappable counter cards with age context, who's-coming shows guest avatars (registered ones locked-checked), and the lead guest keeps its gold Crown.

Liquid glass

Pre-auth gets the branded gradient + a desktop split

Now in code: auth is a lone ~326px card on a flat light background, shown the same on desktop as on mobile (the header logo only appears above the laptop breakpoint) — no depth, no brand moment at the very first screen.

Proposed: the mobile card frosts (liquid glass) over the brand gradient with color orbs — the same pre-auth treatment as Find-booking / Error — and desktop becomes a proper brand-panel split (gradient hero + value props beside the form) instead of a stranded narrow card.

Find booking

V2 · Liquid glass

The property is the hero, not the form

Now in code: SearchReservation is a form on an empty page. Nothing on it says which home you are checking into — the guest's first ever impression of the property is a title, two radios and a disabled button.

Proposed / in V2: a photographic hero canvas (painted in CSS, so it never 404s and can be re-tinted per brand) carries the property name, the city, the "Official check-in" seal, the host and their rating. The search floats over it on a frosted sheet lifted -34px into the photo. On desktop the canvas takes the full left half and stays put through every step — search, wait, result. The screen finally looks like the front door of a specific place.

V2 · Input

The booking code gets the shape of the answer

Now in code: one anonymous Input labelled "Booking reference code". Length unknown, format unknown; a typo is a wall of characters to re-scan.

Proposed / in V2: eight segmented cells split HMAK · 2931, so the shape of the code is visible before you type it and a wrong character is locatable at a glance. A caret walks the cells, filled cells pop, a Paste chip fills all eight at once, and the primary button stays disabled until the last cell lands.

V2 · Waiting

The wait shows the property being matched

Now in code: SearchingBookingModal is a spinner and the words "Searching booking…". The guest has no idea what is being searched, or how far along it is.

Proposed / in V2: a mini property card with a scan line sweeping over it, above a three-row checklist that ticks through Reading your reference → Matching the reservation → Preparing your check-in. Same two seconds, but they now read as progress against a named thing rather than dead air.

V2 · Payoff

"Booking found" becomes something worth finding

Now in code: the success state is the same modal with its text swapped to "Booking found!", then an immediate redirect. The one moment the guest is owed a reward passes in a second, showing nothing.

Proposed / in V2: a boarding pass — property strip, check-in and check-out dates with the night count between them, a copyable reference, the lead guest and party size, a QR block. Tear notches are masked out of the card so it reads as a real ticket on any background. The guest sees the stay we matched, and can check it's the right one before continuing.

Liquid glass

Two glass versions, side-by-side switchable

Now in code: the pre-auth screens (search, errors) are flat solid cards with lots of empty margin — no considered treatment of depth or brand at the moment the guest first lands.

Proposed / in the prototype: a "Glass style: Full · Minimal" toggle on the Find-booking & Error pages. Full frosts the floating auth card, method cards, property chip and language pill over color orbs; Minimal reverts those to solids and keeps only baseline chrome glass. The choice persists across pages so the two can be compared directly — a decision aid, not a shipped feature.

Clarity

Search methods become self-explaining cards

Now in code: SearchReservation shows two bare radio labels ("Booking Reference" / "Check-in Date & Email") and a disabled Continue — you select, then press, then land on a form. No hint of what each method needs.

Proposed: two descriptive cards (icon, one-line explainer, and "what you'll need" — e.g. HMAK-2931 / No code needed) that advance on one tap, folding the redundant Continue press into the choice. Same two routes (bookingCode / checkingDate), one fewer tap.

Trust

The property you're checking into, shown up front

Now in code: every search screen just repeats the title "Find your Booking" — the guest gets no confirmation they're on the right property's link (a stale or wrong link only fails after a search).

Proposed: a property-context chip (name · city · "Official") from the already-loaded useHousing data on the method screen, and a compact version in the sub-screen top bars — a wrong link is caught before a single field is typed.

Guidance

"Where do I find my booking code?" actually answered

Now in code: BookingCodeView is a single plain text field labelled "Booking reference code" — guests who don't know the code have nowhere to turn but to abandon.

Proposed: a big uppercase mono field with an example plus a "Where do I find this?" sheet (confirmation email · booking platform · or switch to date + email) and an inline "use date & email instead" escape — right where guests get stuck.

Recovery

"Not found" becomes next steps, not a dead end

Now in code: ReservationNotFoundModal is a warning icon, two lines of text and a single OK that dumps the guest back where they started with no idea what to try.

Proposed: an actionable state — check the code & retry, try the other method, contact your host (and, where canCreate allows, register a new stay → NewReservationView). Each a tappable row, so a miss has an exit.

Efficiency

Create-reservation form: grouped + "From your search" tags

Now in code: NewReservationView (property link) is a flat 6-field list; the values it silently preloaded from your search (startDate, email) look identical to the empty ones.

Proposed: two labelled groups — Your stay (dates, guests) then Lead guest (name, email) — with a green "From your search" tag on the carried-over fields, so guests only fill what's genuinely missing.

Reassurance

The wait is a branded search, not a bare spinner

Now in code: SearchingBookingModal shows centered text — "Searching booking…" then "Booking found!" — with no visual anchor.

Proposed: a radar-pulse around a magnifier for the LOADING state and a pop-in check for SUCCESS (with a booking summary + auto-redirect note). Same two StatusModalStates, made calm and legible. The DirectReservationView deep-link reuses the same searching state.

Identity verification

Orientation
