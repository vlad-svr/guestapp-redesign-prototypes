What changed and why

Each improvement maps to a concrete gap found in the current redesign-2.0 code.

Orientation

Segmented progress bar in IV & registration

Now in code: progress is text only — "Step X of Y" as the top-bar subtitle (IVTopBarSubtitle); the mobile stepper was removed.

Proposed: a slim 4px segmented bar under the glass top bar. Text stays for accessibility; the bar gives instant "how much is left" without reading.

Liquid glass

Glass chrome: top bar, bottom nav, camera pills

Now in code: solid white top bar and bottom nav; solid white status pill floating over the camera.

Proposed: backdrop-blur glass for the sticky chrome and for on-camera status/hint pills. Content scrolls visibly underneath, chrome feels lighter, and on the dark camera view glass pills read better than solid white. Used only on chrome — never on reading surfaces.

Focus

"Next up" spotlight on the checklist

Now in code: the pending row gets a blue badge ring, but all rows carry equal visual weight.

Proposed: the next task gets a left accent bar + soft blue wash + pulsing badge + "Next up" chip, and completed rows show outcome facts ("€ 380.00 paid"). One glance answers "what do I do now?".

Trust

Time estimates & privacy reassurance in IV

Now in code: the IV intro lists steps but gives no duration and no data-privacy message; camera permission and selfie steps are where guests drop off.

Proposed: "Takes about 2 minutes" up front, a GDPR privacy note on the intro, and "selfie is blurred & encrypted" right on the liveness screen — reassurance at the exact moment of hesitation.

Guidance

Live hint chips around the existing camera card

Now in code: camera sits in a card inside the standard light page; guidance is one static status pill.

Proposed: keep the page and camera card exactly as they are; add hint chips under the frame — "Good lighting · No glare · All corners visible" — that can flip to green checks as conditions are met. Capture success turns the existing brackets green and shows quality confirmations before moving on.

Guidance

Directional liveness cues + micro-steps

Now in code: Lottie icon + a text line below the camera; a 15s timeout then a red failure overlay.

Proposed: an animated arrow pill anchored to the face oval ("Turn your head left") plus a 4-dot challenge tracker, so guests see progress inside the liveness check instead of guessing how many prompts remain.

Momentum

Completion screen that sells the next step

Now in code: a modal sheet with an 80px illustration and "verification is completed" text.

Proposed: a full celebration screen: green ring, a receipt card of what was verified (document + face match), and a primary CTA "Confirm my details" that carries momentum into the pre-filled form.

Efficiency

Auto-filled field tags on the review form

Now in code: the title flips to "Review your details" but pre-filled fields look identical to manual ones.

Proposed: an "8 fields auto-filled" summary banner plus per-field ✦ auto tags and green borders. Guests skim what came from the passport and jump straight to the 2 fields that actually need typing.

Collaboration

Share-link for co-travellers

Now in code: one device registers everyone; a companion's data must be typed by the booker.

Proposed: a "Not together right now?" banner on the hub that shares a registration link, letting each guest scan their own document on their own phone.

Motivation

Arrival countdown & instant-keys incentive

Now in code: the hero shows dates/times; the reason to finish check-in early is implicit.

Proposed: a glass countdown strip in the hero — "Check-in opens in 3 days — finish 2 tasks to get your keys instantly" — tying tasks to the reward (virtual keys), which also appears as a "Locked" state on the keys card.

Clarity

Document picker with effort hints

Now in code: RadioCardList shows document names only.

Proposed: each option states its cost — "Single-sided · fastest" vs "Front and back required" — with a ⚡ Recommended badge on passport, nudging guests to the least error-prone path.

Liquid glass

Sticky glass CTA dock

Now in code: the Continue button sits in the page flow — on long forms it's below the fold.

Proposed: the primary action lives in a sticky dock with a gradient-fade glass background, always reachable by thumb, with an "progress is saved automatically" reassurance where relevant.

Implementation notes. Everything here uses existing token values (@chekinapp/tokens) — no new colors were invented. The glass effect is backdrop-filter: blur() saturate() over existing surfaces with graceful fallback (solid rgba backgrounds) and it is confined to chrome: top bars, bottom nav, on-camera pills, CTA docks. All motion respects prefers-reduced-motion. The segmented progress, accent-bar "next up" row, and auto-fill tags are pure CSS additions to existing components (IVTopBarSubtitle → progress segments, TaskRow, GuestFormFields).
