IV QR — verify at the door (Mobile)

Identity verification at an on-property housing must prove one thing: the guest is standing at the door right now.
The entrance QR. The guest scans the sticker, lands on the SDK page (pages/VerifyOnHousing → pages/FindReservationsByName, mode QR_IV_ON_PROPERTY), finds their booking by name, and is redirected into their check-in session to run the regular IV flow. The scan at the door was the proof.
The in-app gate. Whenever that proof is missing, IVQRGuard holds IV shut and sends the guest to IVQRScannerView to scan the entrance sticker from inside the app. Either way IV only opens on the check-in date — otherwise IVUnavailableView. Mock data only.

Why verify at the property?

Spain requires hosts to register every guest with the authorities, and to confirm the guest really is there.

Presence is proved exactly once. Scanning the entrance code — either to open this page, or from inside the app — is what proves it.
Your host never sees your document. It's encrypted and sent only to the police registry that requires it.
The selfie is deleted after matching. It confirms the document belongs to you, then it's gone.
Why one more detail?

Two people staying here tonight share your name. If we guessed, one of you would end up scanning an ID into the other's reservation.

We ask for the smallest thing that separates you — usually a document number, sometimes a birth date. Never more than one at a time.
Use your check-in link

If your host emailed you a check-in link, open that instead — it knows your reservation, so there's nothing to search for. You'll scan this same entrance QR from inside the app before your ID.
