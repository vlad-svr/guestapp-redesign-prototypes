Splash screen — the arrival moment (Mobile)

The splash is a handoff, not a gate. A rebuild of components/SplashScreen, clocked. The shipped component holds the guest for load + 5.0 s, closes two curtains over its own logo at 4.3 s, and — if the auth request hangs — never leaves. Here it covers the skeleton of the screen it is about to become, leaves the instant the data lands, and when the data never lands it says so and offers a way out. The surface stays V1's; everything that changes is behaviour.

The timing contract

500 ms minimum — a splash that flashes for 90 ms is worse than none.
1.2 s before any text — narrating a fast load is noise.
6 s → admit it. Elapsed clock, Try again, and a promise that nothing is lost.
20 s → watchdog. Stop pretending; render the error state with real exits.
Today: load + 5000 ms, with no upper bound at all.
