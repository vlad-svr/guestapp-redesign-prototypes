What changed and why

Status pill tells a story

Same banner slot, but it now names the guest (“Sending Ana's details…”), explains you can keep browsing, and morphs to green with overall progress (“2 of 3 guests done”) — instead of a generic grey “sending your data”.

“Skip” is never red

Today's skip button uses the destructive variant — red signals errors, not “no thanks”. Renamed to “Not now” with a neutral ghost style; “Skip all” became “Skip offers — continue”, which says where you'll land.

Progress you can count

Dots became labeled segments (“1 of 3”), and a persistent cart chip shows what you've added so far — so accepting an offer has visible, reversible consequences (“nothing charged yet”).

Non-blocking swipe hint

The full-screen swipe tutorial overlay became a small animated glass chip on the first card — buttons stay tappable, and users who never swipe are not interrupted.

No dead empty state

When the host has no offers, guests get a pure success moment with the next step (“Verify Carlos's ID”) — never “no deals available”, which reads as an error at the happiest point of the flow.

Errors keep your work

The error state explains what went wrong in human words, keeps the technical detail secondary, and reassures that the typed data is safe — with “Try again” and “Edit details” instead of a lone “OK”.
